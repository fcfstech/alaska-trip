Heat is the story now, not storms. The wet pattern cleared out — both days are hot, sunny, and humid with highs in the mid-to-upper 90s and a heat index well over 100°F by afternoon. Rain chances are near zero. Fish the cool early hours hard, then get off the water or into shade by early afternoon.
Wind: Monday's south wind (~19 mph, gusting higher) will stack a chop on open water — the long run to the dam is doable but bumpy. Tuesday is calmer (~14 mph), making it the better day for a longer run or fishing exposed main-lake structure.
Stay safe in the heat: bring far more water than you think you need, electrolytes, sun shirt, hat, and high-SPF sunscreen. UV index is 10 (very high) both days. Watch yourself and anyone aboard for cramps, dizziness, or nausea.

🚤 Launch — Coming From the Southwest

Coming from the SW, Cedar Mills is the efficient call — it sits on the Texas side at the southwest end, so you skip a long cross-lake run. The river-channel structure right off the ramp holds plenty of fish this time of year.

The ships store opens later in the morning — after your pre-dawn launch. Handle your launch fee, parking pass, and ice the evening before, or call 903-523-4222 ahead. Confirm current launch cost & ramp conditions at cedarmills.com.

Alternative (classic mid-lake / dam grounds)

Highport / Lighthouse (Pottsboro) or Grandpappy Point (Denison) sit near the famous striper water by the dam — but they're a good hour-plus farther east. From the SW, Cedar Mills is the smart launch.

🪣 Bait — Where to Launch the Bait Hunt

Bait can be the hard part this time of year. Catching shad has been tough lately. Two routes: net your own early near Cedar Mills, or call a marina ahead to confirm live shad before you drive over.

1) Net your own (fastest & free)

Throw a cast net early for threadfin/gizzard shad in shaded cove pockets, bridge pilings, and creek mouths near deeper water around Cedar Mills. Use your graph to find the school first, then throw. Ease in on the trolling motor — the big motor scatters the school.

33.8550, −96.8000
#1 summer pattern. Drop live shad to ~30 ft over 40–45 ft of water on the channel edges. Move every 30–45 min if no marks.
🏞️
Denison Dam — deep summer fish MIDDAY
33.8314, −96.5723
Summer stripers stack in deep, cooler water near the dam — a strong midday play when surface fish shut off in the heat. Slabs or live shad in 40–65 ft. Long run from Cedar Mills; smoother on calmer Tuesday.
🐦
Diving seagulls — anywhere RUN & GUN
Watch the open water
Birds = stripers pushing shad up. Run to them; topwater or shad burned just under the surface.
🎯 Game plan: first light topwater on points → mid-morning drop live shad on channel edges (40–45 ft) → midday go deep near the dam as the surface bite dies in the heat → chase birds when they pop. The evening cool-down can trigger a second feed.
Heat & wind note: Monday's ~19 mph south wind chops up open water — fish protected channel structure or pick your crossings. Tuesday's lighter wind is friendlier for the long dam run. Either day, the midday heat is brutal — deep water and shade are your friends.

🎯 Rigs & Lures

Live Bait Rig

14–20 lb mono · Carolina rig: egg sinker → heavy swivel → leader → circle hook. Let the fish load up — don't swing hard.

Artificial Backup

5" fluke (baby bass) · 4" sassy shad (white/glow or chartreuse) on a 3/4–1 oz white jighead · swimbaits on points · slab spoons fished vertically over schooling fish.

📜 Regulations — Texoma Is Special

License
Lake Texoma License Endorsement (~$12)
Striper Limit
10/day — no more than 2 over 20"
Texoma is a border lake. The endorsement lets you fish both TX + OK water on one license. A standard Texas license covers Texas water only. Know the limit before you cull — it's different from other Texas lakes.
