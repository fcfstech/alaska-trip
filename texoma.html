Heads up
Store opens AFTER launch
⚠The store opens at 8 a.m. — after your pre-dawn launch. Handle your launch fee, parking pass, and ice the evening before, or call 903-523-4222 ahead.
Alternative (classic mid-lake / dam grounds)
Highport Marina (Pottsboro) or Grandpappy Point (Denison) sit near the famous striper water by the dam — but they're a good hour-plus farther east of I-35. For a day trip off I-35, Cedar Mills is the efficient call and the west end holds plenty of fish this time of year.
🪣 Bait — Catch Your Own Shad
⚠Don't count on buying live bait. Cedar Mills' live-bait references are tied to guide charters, not retail, and the store opens after you launch. Plan to cast-net your own shad. Call 903-523-4222 Thursday to confirm if they sell shad and when.
Where & When
Throw the cast net before daylight at marina/dock lights and creek & cove mouths next to deeper water. Use the trolling motor to ease in — the big motor spooks and scatters the school.
Cast Net
8 ft · ~3/8" mesh · ~3/4 lb/ft
Keep Bait Alive
Aerator + salt + block ice + defoamer
🧊Water's ~82°F — heat kills bait fast. Block ice in the tank is the #1 thing that makes or breaks a live-bait day.

🎯Game plan: First light = flats/channel edges & any surface action. Once the sun's up, slide to deeper channel ledges (20–40 ft) and graph-and-drop live shad onto the schools you mark. Move every 30–45 min if no marks or bites.
🎯 Rigs & Lures
Live Bait Rig
14–20 lb mono · Carolina rig
Egg sinker → heavy swivel → leader → circle hook. Let the fish load up — don't swing hard.
Artificial Backup
For surface action or if bait fails
5" Zoom Fluke (Baby Bass) · 4" sassy shad (white/glow or chartreuse) on 3/4–1 oz white jighead · swimbaits on points.
📜 Regulations — Texoma is Special
License
Lake Texoma License Endorsement (~$12)
Texoma is a border lake. The endorsement lets you fish both TX + OK water on one license. A standard Texas license covers Texas water only.
Striper Limit (Texoma-specific)
10 per day — no more than 2 over 20"
Different from other Texas lakes. Know it before you cull.
